Becoming A Coder Doesn't Require Prior Computer Programming Experience

( If You Have The Will, There's A Way With LaunchCode )

LaunchCode Came up During A Friendly Conversation

My journey into coding began with a friendly conversation with the father of one of my clients. We were discussing lucrative skill trades and he mentioned something about coding being an underrated, high-demand skill that offerred a variety of profitable career opportunities. I had previously heard the term "coding" in several cheesy YouTube ads, but I hadn't taken it seriously until he shared with me that he earned over $70K his first year of working for a tech company developing geospatial websites for farmers. I congratulated him for being the stereotypical computer nerd, like the scoially awkward movie character who eventually earns a lot of money and ends up marrying a woman who's far better looking than him. We shared a laugh, and then I went on to explain that I kwew diddly-squat about computers and that the Department of Education was already bullying my pockets, so I couldn't afford to go back to college. He chunckled, and then told me the organization where he works frequently hires apprentices from a free, non-profit, technology training program called LaunchCode.

LaunchCode's Enrollment Process

The next day, I walked into the LaunchCode headquarters and spoke with the director and several LaunchCode workers to get information about the training they offered and the enrollment requirements. To my delight, I didn't need any computer programming experience to enroll, all I needed was a basic laptop, and if I didn't have one, LaunchCode issued laptops to students to complete the course at no additional cost. More over from that, I didn't have to worry about the courses conflicting with my current work schedule, because LaunchCode offered a free, part-time web development course that met twice a week after 5PM. The timing couldn't had been more perfect. Applying for enrollment online was easy, and in a few weeks, I was accepted and enrolled in LaunchCode's free Web Development course.

LaunchCode Has A Diverse Network Of Coders

Since starting at LaunchCode, not only have I learned the basic computer skills related to writing and repairing code in JavaScript, but equally as important, I've also learned the value of establishing computer programming networks with peers. LaunchCode has a diverse student population that consist of various races and cultures who have an assortment of computer programming skills. For example, some students, such as myself, came into the program with little to no coding experience, while others had previously taken coding courses before, or have relatives who work for cyber security. The class structure at LaunchCode is mixed in such a way that if a student struggles to understand a code method or concept, not only can he or she get assistance from a TA, but the student can also interact with colleagues who have slightly more coding experience to figure out solutions. There's a sense of camaraderie when I look to my left or right and notice someone encountering the same complication I'm having, because it shows me that I'm not going through the challenges and frustrations associated with learning code alone, and if need be, there's a network of knowledgable peers to reach out to for help. If you're reading this, that means I have successfully developed and published my first webpage with the training offered by LaunchCode. This webpage is a testament that you don't need any prior experience in computer programming to become a coder. If you have the will, there's a way.
